## Cardinals Dynasty (1941–1960)

### Championship Events
- 1942-1946: 5 straight pennants; 3 straight WS titles
- 1944: "Streetcar Series" — both St. Louis teams in WS
- 1951: Giants — Bobby Thomson walk-off HR
- 1955: Dodgers — Brooklyn's lone WS title
- 1958: Dodgers move to LA; Giants move to SF

### Key Records
- Most consecutive pennants: Cardinals — 5 (1942-1946)
- Most WS titles in an era: Cardinals — 6 (1941-1967)

---

## Expansion Era (1961–1980)

### Championship Events
- 1962: Giants — 1st pennant in SF; 162-game schedule begins
- 1969: Mets — Miracle 100-win WS champion; NL expands to 12 teams
- 1975-1976: Reds — Back-to-back WS titles (Big Red Machine)
- 1979: Pirates — WS champion

### Key Records
- 1969 Mets: 100-62 expansion team wins WS
- 1975-76 Reds: First NL back-to-back WS titles since 1920s

## Braves Dynasty (1981–2000)

### Championship Events
- 1991-2005: Braves — 14 consecutive division titles (MLB record)
- 1994: Expos — Best record in MLB (74-40) when strike hit
- 1995: Braves — WS champion (swept Indians)
- 1997: Marlins — 2nd-year expansion team wins WS

### Key Records
- Longest division title streak: Braves — 14 (1991-2005)

## Resurgence Cycle (2001–2015)

### Championship Events
- 2001: D-backs — Wild card team wins dramatic WS Game 7
- 2006: Cardinals — Lowest regular-season wins (83) to win WS since 1973
- 2010-2014: Giants — 3 titles in 5 years
- 2016: Cubs — WS champion (ended 108-year drought)

### Key Records
- 2006 Cardinals: 83 wins (lowest to win WS since 1973)
- 2016 Cubs: Ended 108-year WS drought

## Analytical Summary

### The Three Pillars of NL Dominance
1. **St. Louis Cardinals** — Longest sustained dynasty; 11 WS titles; 19 pennants
2. **Los Angeles Dodgers** — Modern era dominance; 8 straight NL West; 9 WS titles; 26 pennants
3. **San Francisco Giants** — Highest all-time win%; 8 WS titles; 23 pennants

### The Longest Droughts
1. Cubs — 108 years (1908-2016)
2. Pirates — 34 years (1979-2013)
3. Padres — 38 years (1984-2022)
4. Giants/SF — 56 years (1954-2010)
5. Reds — 14 years (1976-1990)
