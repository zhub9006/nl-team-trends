### 2. Era-by-Era Trends

#### Pre-Modern Era (1876-1903)
- Original NL had 8 teams; no divisions
- Champions determined by best win-loss record in a single table
- Boston/Cleveland/Chicago/New York Giants dominated early
- No World Series until 1903
- Key trend: No franchise had sustained dominance until the 1880s

#### Dead-Ball Era (1904-1919)
- Record-setting wins: Cubs (116 in 1906, 104 in 1907, 104 in 1910)
- Giants dynasty: 1903-1905, 1911-1913, 1917, 1921-1924 (8 pennants in 22 years)
- Boston Miracle (1914): Worst-to-first World Series champs
- World Series began in 1903; NL won 5 of first 19 (1903-1919)

#### Live-Ball Era (1920-1945)
- Cardinals rise: 1926 WS, 1931 WS, 1934 WS, 1942-1944 WS triple
- Yankees era: NL won only 1 WS (1919) during this period (Cardinals)
- Cardinals became the NL's most successful franchise
- Key trend: Cardinals established permanent dominance

#### Post-War Expansion (1946-1969)
- Dodgers relocation (1958): Brooklyn → Los Angeles
- Giants relocation (1958): NY → San Francisco
- Senators-to-Mets (1962): 100+ win Miracle Mets
- Big Red Machine: 1970s Reds dynasty (3 WS titles)
- Key trend: Geographic relocation reshaped NL

#### Divisional Era (1970-1992)
- 3-division format (1969): NL East, NL West, NL Central (added later)
- Pittsburgh and Cincinnati dominated early
- 1969 Mets: First expansion team to win WS
- Key era: Reds (1970s), Cardinals (late 1970s-1980s)

#### Braves Dynasty (1993-2005)
- Atlanta won 14 consecutive division titles
- Records for sustained divisional dominance
- 1995 WS championship behind Greg Maddux/Tom Glavine/John Smoltz
- Key trend: Most dominant single-franchise era in modern MLB

#### Modern Era (2006-2025)
- Resurgent Cardinals: 2006, 2011 WS titles; 3 pennants this era
- Crazy Cubs: 2015 NLCS, 2016 WS after 108-year drought
- Dodgers dynasty: 8 straight NL West titles (2013-2025), 3 pennants
- Key trend: LA Dodgers became most consistently dominant club

### 3. Win-Loss Record Patterns

- Champions typically achieve .600+ WPct over full season
