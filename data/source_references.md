## Data Collection Methodology

- All-time franchise records compiled from Baseball Reference and Baseball Almanac seasonal standings
- Championship history cross-referenced across multiple sources
- Win-loss percentages use standard formula: Wins / (Wins + Losses)
- Wild Card and tie-breaker results noted where applicable
- Some pre-1900 records may include different game counts (120-154 vs 162)
- Milwaukee Brewers moved from AL to NL Central in 1998
- Montreal Expos became Washington Nationals in 2005

## Caveats

- Pre-1969 seasons used 154-game schedules (except 1876-1892 which varied)
- 1994 season was strike-shortened; no NL pennant awarded
- 2020 season was 60-game COVID-shortened season
- Expansion eras (1969, 1993, 1998, 2005) changed competitive dynamics
- Win% is the primary fairness metric when comparing across different schedule lengths
