# NL Team Trends — Data Sources & Methodology

## Primary Sources

1. **Baseball-Reference.com** — https://www.baseball-reference.com/leagues/NL/
   Comprehensive league and team encyclopedias with season-by-season records.

2. **Baseball Almanac** — https://www.baseball-almanac.com/
   NL team-vs-team head-to-head win-loss matrix (1876–present).

3. **StatMuse** — https://www.statmuse.com/
   All-time franchise win-loss records and aggregate statistics.

4. **Lahman Baseball Database (SABR)** — https://github.com/chadwickbureau/baseballdatabank
   Complete historical batting/pitching/fielding stats back to 1871.
   License: Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0

5. **MLB.com** — https://www.mlb.com/standings
   Official MLB standings and statistics.

## Secondary Sources

- **Wikipedia** — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_League_(baseball)
  Franchise histories, era summaries, World Series results.
- **The Athletic / Baseball Prospectus / Hardball Times**
  Analytical trend pieces and historical retrospectives.

## Methodology Notes

- All records are **regular-season only**; postseason is excluded.
- Pre-1920 ties are excluded from Win% calculations (Win% = W / (W + L)).
- Franchise relocations treated as continuous entities (e.g., Brooklyn Dodgers = LA Dodgers).
- Era definitions:
  - Founding Era: 1876–1900
  - Dead-Ball Era: 1901–1919
  - Live-Ball Golden Age: 1920–1960
  - Expansion Era: 1961–1992
  - Wild Card/Interleague Era: 1993–2025

## Key Caveats

- Pre-1900 records are less reliable due to incomplete documentation.
- Colorado Rockies' .455 Win% is influenced by Coors Field altitude (1993–2018).
- 1994 season was cut short by player strike; no official champion crowned.
